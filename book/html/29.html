Then our approximation (2.13) will have been a poor one, and we might ﬁnd that the energy E(w) increases (instead of decreasing ) after we use  to update w. To avoid such situations, several methods to modify  exist. One such a modiﬁcation is the Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm, which replaces (2.14) by  =  Ψ T Ψ + λI  -1 Ψ T τ , (2.15) where λ ≥ 0 is a tunable parameter that is typically updated after each iteration. If the parameter update  does not decrease E(w), the update is rejected and λ is increased until a decrease in E(w) is obtained; this will happen eventually because for very large λ the algorithm devolves into a gradient-descent algorithm with a small step size. Conversely, if E(w) decreases a smaller λ is used in the next iteration of the algorithm to improve eﬃciency.
