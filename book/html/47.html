Combined with the guarantee of increasingly better estimates of the maximum likelihood parameters as iterations progress, it should be clear why this algorithm is a popular choice for maximum likelihood estimation of Gaussian mixture model parameters. The trick exploited by the EM algorithm to construct its lower bound is based on the property of the logarithm that it is a concave function, i.e., every chord connecting two points on its curve lies on or below that curve (see Fig. 3.6). Mathematically, this means that log [wx 1 + (1 - w)x 2 ] ≥ w log x 1 + (1 - w) log x 2 for any two points x 1 and x 2 and 0 ≤ w ≤ 1.
